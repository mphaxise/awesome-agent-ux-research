# Products And Landscape

This file tracks product-side market signals relevant to AI agent UX and UX research for AI agents.

## Synthetic testing and UX research

- [Uxia](https://www.uxia.app/)
  - What it is: AI user testing with synthetic testers and fast mission-based reports.
  - Why it matters: one of the clearest direct signals that synthetic UX testing is becoming a product category.

- [Crowdi](https://www.crowdi.org/)
  - What it is: simulated AI users running through products to find bugs, friction, and conversion problems.
  - Why it matters: shows the market bending toward scaled simulated usage, not only classic human research.

- [Loop11 AI Browser Agents](https://www.loop11.com/)
  - What it is: AI browser agents inside an established usability testing platform.
  - Why it matters: strong evidence that AI participants are being layered into existing UX research workflows, not only sold as a net-new category.

## Human research substitutes and complements

- [UserTesting](https://www.usertesting.com/)
  - What it is: large human insight platform for studies, interviews, and feedback.
  - Why it matters: still a core substitute when trust in synthetic users is too low.

- [UXArmy](https://uxarmy.com/)
  - What it is: human usability testing and research workflows for websites, apps, and prototypes.
  - Why it matters: shows the incumbent workflow that AI-first tools are trying to compress.

## Browser and QA infrastructure

- [Browserbase](https://www.browserbase.com/)
  - What it is: cloud browser infrastructure for agents.
  - Why it matters: browser execution is turning into infrastructure, which means UX differentiation has to come from judgment and synthesis.

- [Stagehand](https://www.stagehand.dev/)
  - What it is: agent-friendly browser automation layer.
  - Why it matters: lowers the cost of building evaluation or testing workflows around real interfaces.

- [QA Wolf](https://www.qawolf.com/platform)
  - What it is: hybrid end-to-end QA platform and service.
  - Why it matters: useful comparison for service-plus-software trust models.

## App-building agents and demand-side growth

- [Replit Agent](https://replit.com/ai)
  - What it is: natural-language app and site generation with deployment.
  - Why it matters: increases the number of generated apps that need quality review.

- [v0](https://v0.dev/)
  - What it is: fast app and UI generation from prompts.
  - Why it matters: pushes design and implementation faster than most teams can validate experience quality.

- [Lovable](https://lovable.dev/)
  - What it is: AI app builder for creating apps and websites by chatting.
  - Why it matters: strong demand-side signal that agent-built interfaces are becoming normal enough to justify a dedicated research layer.

## Open questions to track here

- Which of these tools are actually trusted in release workflows?
- Which are framed as UX research versus QA versus release confidence?
- Which expose workflows that could be made agent-callable?
